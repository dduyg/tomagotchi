⚞Diffusion models are a class of generative models that simulate the gradual process of adding noise to data and then learn to reverse this process to generate new, high-quality data samples. During training, noise is incrementally added to the data over multiple steps until it resembles pure noise. The model then learns to denoise it step-by-step, effectively generating realistic data samples from noise. These models have shown state-of-the-art performance in generating high-quality images, text, and other data types.
