Title: "Love Type Error"

Premise:

Baek Ji-won, a by-the-book HR manager at a major cosmetics company, is forced into a fake relationship with Choi Hyun-woo, a free-spirited, notoriously flirtatious product developer, to protect the company’s reputation after they're caught in a compromising misunderstanding. As they pretend to be a couple to calm the gossip mill, real feelings sneak in—and chaos follows.

Episode 1: “Caught on Camera”

Baek Ji-won and Choi Hyun-woo get stuck in the office elevator after hours. Security footage shows them falling on top of each other—literally—and gossip spreads like wildfire. To avoid fueling dating rumors that could affect their professional standings, they’re “advised” by the CEO to pretend to be in a relationship. They both hate the idea.


Episode 2: “Clause 1: No Kissing”

Ji-won drafts a hilariously specific “Fake Relationship Contract,” complete with no-kissing and no-eye-contact clauses. Hyun-woo mocks her rules but signs it anyway. They start faking affection at work, including awkward lunch dates and “candid” couple selfies. But when Hyun-woo unexpectedly defends her from a rude executive, Ji-won sees a new side of him.

Episode 3: “Fake First Date”

To sell the illusion, they go on a “first date” at a trendy rooftop café. Ji-won prepares bullet points. Hyun-woo brings a deck of cheesy pickup lines. The evening is a disaster—until it isn’t. After sharing embarrassing childhood stories, they end the night laughing. Ji-won smiles genuinely for the first time in ages.


Episode 4: “Jealousy Protocol”

Hyun-woo flirts with a visiting intern to make Ji-won jealous. It backfires when Ji-won coolly approves of their “chemistry.” Later, Ji-won runs into her ex-fiancé at a company event. Hyun-woo steps in as her charming fake boyfriend, leaving the ex stunned. Ji-won’s heart skips, unsure if it’s relief… or something more.

Episode 5: “The Accidental Sleepover”

A company retreat forces them to share a room. Ji-won insists on building a pillow barrier. Hyun-woo secretly watches her sleep-talking about how “he’s not that bad.” The next morning, she wakes up to find him making her coffee—just the way she likes it. She’s flustered. He’s smug. The lines are blurring.


Episode 6: “Office Scandal 2.0”

Photos of them looking too affectionate leak online. The PR team spins it as a “company love story,” pushing them into the spotlight. Now, they're expected to appear as the perfect couple at a press event. Ji-won panics under the pressure, but Hyun-woo keeps her calm. He’s not just faking it anymore—and neither is she.

Episode 7: “A Real Confession, Almost”

Hyun-woo almost confesses his growing feelings—but stops when Ji-won refers to their relationship as “purely strategic.” He puts on a happy face, but becomes more distant. Ji-won senses the shift, misses his teasing, and begins questioning what she truly wants. Meanwhile, a new team member shows interest in her—and Hyun-woo doesn’t like it.


Episode 8: “The Break Clause”

Ji-won suggests ending the contract early, convinced they’ve fulfilled their “duties.” Hyun-woo agrees, acting unaffected. But both struggle with the breakup—even if it was fake. Ji-won can’t focus, while Hyun-woo’s new product flops. They’re miserable apart but too stubborn to admit why. Meanwhile, their coworkers notice—and plot to push them back together.
