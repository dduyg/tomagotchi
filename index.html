⚞Diffusion models are a class of generative models that simulate the gradual process of adding noise to data and then learn to reverse this process to generate new, high-quality data samples. During training, noise is incrementally added to the data over multiple steps until it resembles pure noise. The model then learns to denoise it step-by-step, effectively generating realistic data samples from noise. These models have shown state-of-the-art performance in generating high-quality images, text, and other data types, rivaling or surpassing previous techniques like Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). Beyond generation, diffusion models are being explored for tasks such as data augmentation, anomaly detection, and predictive modeling, making them versatile tools in modern data science.
